The Leech

An exploration in product design using everyday items.

To create an object of the future with a purpose out of several other objects - all from the dollar store.

Ideation

This project was a strange one, even for me. Take a handful of objects and put them together, and then derive a purpose from it, that was our task. As a student I'm rather cheap, so I started by looking around at the things I have that I can use (And possibly destroy). A glass candle holder, a toilet paper roll, an old plastic fork, a pair of takeout chopsticks, a plastic chip bag clip.

When I ran out of things, I went with a peer to the dollar store and we brainstormed. "This has lots of potential, lots of holes to stick other things into!" I picked up a lot of items I thought I could use - condoms, a toilet paper roll, some plastic shot glasses, plastic kitchen dish scrubbies, and some suction cups with little hooks on them.

The next part was looking at each item and categorising it: what was its original purpose? What does it feel like? Is there anything interesting about it? What colour is it?

❮ ❯

Prototypes

I went through 9 different prototypes before I settled on my final. I tried having different items as the 'base' and got some weird combinations - my favourite that I didn't end up using was a kitchen scrubby with a fork stuck in it, and suction cups hanging around the edge. I couldn't find a purpose for it, so I wasn't able to use it as the final product.
